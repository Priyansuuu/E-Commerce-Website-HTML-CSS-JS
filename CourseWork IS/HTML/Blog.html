Caring for a Bonsai during a Heatwave

November 11 2022

During high temperatures,Bonsai needs an extra care. We must provide shadow to it and water carefully as much as possible.

Deeply buried in the ground, the roots of trees growing in the wild are carefully shielded from extreme heat. However, because our bonsai are housed in little pots, the soil's (and the root system's) temperature might rise to risky levels. Pale color of leaves, dieback of conifer branchtips, and fading leaves are some indications that a bonsai is under stress during a heatwave.

“High temperatures of the soil poses the greatest risk to your Bonsai.”

Plant toxicity; keeping Bonsai with pets around

May 18 2022

When pets eat the leaves or fruits of several common tree species used for bonsai, it could be dangerous for them.

When keeping your bonsai indoors with cats or dogs nearby, many starting bonsai enthusiasts worry about tree toxicity. As per the forum survey, it appears that the issues with pets and bonsai are actually very rare.

“Because pets usually don't eat trees, plant toxicity isn't something that Bonsai growers worry about.”
